Solutions to Homelessness

What Seattle is doing...

Seattle's policy framework for addressing homelessness:

  1. Person-centered service model
  2. Invest in programs that effectively connect people to housing
  3. Address racial disparities

Seattle has changed how they are investing in homelessness services. First, they are increasing access to Housing & Diversion. There has been a 10% increase in rent paid without subsidy and 19% increase in people moving into permanent housing after using diversion funds. Second, they're shifting to a Person-Centered Service Model. Seattle has opened 2 new shelters with 175 enhanced shelter beds, enhanced 67% of the 1773 shelter beds, and enhanced 25 shelters. All the shelters have extended hours and supportive services. Third, they are improving government accountability by better training for staff, active contract management, better funding and governance agreements, and competitive funding proccesses for service contracts.
